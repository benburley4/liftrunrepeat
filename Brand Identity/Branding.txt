Core Brand Identity

Brand Name: LiftRunRepeat
(Primary usage: all one word, camel case for readability in logos/headings. Tagline option: "Lift. Run. Repeat." or "Build Strength. Build Stamina. Repeat Daily.")
Positioning: The go-to resource for hybrid athletes — people who refuse to choose between getting strong and getting fast. Programs blend lifting heavy with smart running/endurance work for real-world performance, longevity, and results.
Tone & Voice: Direct, encouraging, no-BS. Motivational without hype. Speak like a knowledgeable training partner: "You've got the plan — now execute. Lift heavy today, run smart tomorrow, repeat forever."

Color Palette
Energetic yet balanced for hybrid (strength = bold/powerful, endurance = fresh/enduring). Inspired by common fitness palettes but tuned for your dual focus.

Primary: Charcoal Black (#1A1A1A or #000000) — for backgrounds, text, strength vibe.
Accent 1 (Strength/Power): Deep Crimson Red (#C8102E or similar fire-engine red) — energy, lifts, calls-to-action.
Accent 2 (Endurance/Flow): Electric Teal/Cyan (#00BFA5 or vibrant turquoise) — running, recovery, progress tracking.
Neutral/Support: Cool Gray (#4A4A4A to #A0A0A0) — secondary text, borders.
Background/Base: Crisp White (#FFFFFF) or very light gray (#F5F5F5) — clean site feel.
Highlight/Warning: Bright Yellow-Orange (#FF9500) — sparingly for progress badges or urgency (e.g., "New Program Drop").

This creates contrast: black/red for heavy lifts sections, teal for run/endurance, blending in hybrid program pages.
Typography

Primary Font (Headings/Logo): Bold sans-serif like Montserrat ExtraBold, Bebas Neue, or Impact-style condensed — strong, uppercase for "LIFT RUN REPEAT" impact.
Secondary Font (Body/Text): Clean readable sans-serif like Inter, Roboto, or Open Sans — for program descriptions, blogs, ease on mobile.
Style Rules:
Headings: UPPERCASE or title case, bold, large tracking (letter spacing).
Body: 16–18px base, line height 1.6 for readability during long workouts.


Logo Concepts (Descriptions — Easy to Brief a Designer)

Primary Logo (Recommended):
Stacked or horizontal: "LIFT" in bold red, "RUN" in teal, "REPEAT" in black/gray below or looping.
Icon: Minimal barbell fused into a running stride/arrow (e.g., barbell plates morph into motion lines). Or a simple cycle arrow ∞ integrated with dumbbell + shoe print.
Keep it scalable: Works in black/white too for merch.

Wordmark Only: Just "LiftRunRepeat" in a custom condensed font, with "Lift" and "Run" slightly bolder/larger, "Repeat" italicized or looped for motion.
Monogram/Icon Variant: "LRR" monogram where the two R's connect in a loop, one stylized as a barbell, one as a running path.

Avoid: Overly cartoonish icons, too many elements — keep it clean for apparel, site favicons, social.
Visual Style & Imagery Guidelines

Photography: Real people mid-lift (squat/deadlift) and mid-run (trail/road), sweaty but determined. Mix gym + outdoor shots. High contrast, dynamic angles.
Graphics: Progress charts (lift PRs + run paces), cycle icons (weekly repeat calendars), split-screen before/after or lift vs run.
UI Elements: Rounded buttons with red/teal gradients, progress bars blending colors, icons of dumbbell + running figure.
Mood Board Vibes: Think Nike Training Club meets Strava — functional, aspirational, community-focused.
